# Amazon US Listing Image Rules Reference

Use this reference as a practical checklist for Amazon US product listing images. Amazon can change policies and category-specific style guides; verify current Seller Central guidance when enforcement risk is high or when the product is in a regulated/sensitive category.

Source note: checked against Amazon's public Sell on Amazon product photography guidance on 2026-05-31, including its stated requirements for at least one image, recommended six images, 500-10,000 px longest side, JPEG/TIFF/PNG/non-animated GIF formats, clear unpixelated images, and white background RGB 255/255/255 guidance.

## Core Technical Requirements

- Image files should be JPEG, PNG, TIFF, or GIF-compatible; avoid animated GIFs for listing image sets.
- Images must be clear, in focus, professionally lit, and accurately represent the product for sale.
- Amazon supports large images for zoom; use 1600 px or larger on the longest side when possible. This skill defaults to 1600 x 1600 px square images.
- Avoid compression artifacts, pixelation, heavy filters, artificial blur, or unrealistic AI rendering artifacts.
- Do not place important content too close to the edges; leave enough margin for Amazon cropping and mobile display.

## Main Image Rules

- Background must be pure white: RGB 255, 255, 255.
- Show the actual product being sold and only what is included in the purchase.
- Product should fill at least 85% of the image frame.
- Show the full product without clipping important edges.
- Do not include people, models, hands, lifestyle props, room scenes, packaging, accessories, inset images, text, graphics, logos, badges, watermarks, borders, star ratings, review snippets, discount language, or promotional claims.
- Do not show multiple variants unless the listing purchase includes all shown items.
- Do not use drawings, mockups, placeholders, or heavily stylized renderings when a real product photo is required for trust and compliance.

## Secondary Image Rules

- Secondary images may use lifestyle scenes, props, people, close-ups, diagrams, and English text callouts if they remain accurate and non-misleading.
- Infographics should use short claims that can be substantiated by the seller.
- Dimension graphics should label real measurements clearly and avoid exaggerating scale.
- Lifestyle images should show realistic use and scale for US buyers.
- Person-in-scene images should look natural and should not imply medical, safety, or performance claims unless documented.
- Avoid text-heavy designs; all text must remain readable on mobile.

## Prohibited Or Risky Content

- Amazon badges, "Amazon's Choice", "Best Seller", Prime icons, star ratings, review counts, coupon badges, or any Amazon UI imitation.
- Competitor comparisons, unsupported superiority claims, false scarcity, fake certifications, or unverifiable guarantees.
- Offensive, obscene, sexually suggestive, violent, unsafe, discriminatory, or illegal content.
- Children/babies in unsafe situations, unsafe installation scenes, or product uses that conflict with instructions.
- Extra accessories, decor, tools, or packaging that buyers might assume are included unless they are included or clearly labeled as not included.
- Medical, food-contact, child-safety, electrical, or structural claims unless the seller provides proof and the category permits the claim.

## Seven-Image Set Checklist

1. Main image: pure white background, product only, no text.
2. Lifestyle with person: realistic scene, natural scale.
3. Dimension image: accurate measurements.
4. Selling-point image: short, substantiated benefits.
5. Detail image: material/craftsmanship/customization close-up.
6. Use-case/customization image: occasions, personalization, placement, or variations.
7. Trust/package/variant image: included parts, packaging, care, installation, or gift presentation.

## Recommended Copy Style For US Buyers

- Prefer concise English phrases: "Personalized Engraving", "Solid Wood", "Easy Wall Mounting", "Gift-Ready Box", "Custom Family Name", "Rust-Resistant Finish".
- Use 3-5 callouts per infographic.
- Avoid absolute claims like "best", "perfect", "guaranteed", "never fades", "100% safe", unless proven and allowed.
- Match the product category and audience: home decor should feel warm and practical; memorial items should be calm and respectful; wedding gifts should feel elegant; rustic products should not look overly polished.
